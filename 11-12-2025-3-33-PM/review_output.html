Understanding Heat Flow

Welcome to the Heat Flow Simulation! This simulation helps you visualize how heat energy moves between objects.

Click the info icons (?) for more details on specific concepts.

Hot and Cold Objects: We perceive objects as "hot" or "cold" based on how heat energy transfers to or from our skin. This feeling is directly related to their temperatures.

Temperature: Temperature is a quantitative measure of "hotness" or "coldness." It represents the average kinetic energy of the particles within an object. We use thermometers to measure it.

Heat Transfer: Heat is energy in transit. It naturally moves from regions of higher temperature to regions of lower temperature. This movement is called the "flow of heat."

Working Principle: Heat flows from a region of higher temperature to a region of lower temperature. This process continues until both objects reach the same temperature, a state known as thermal equilibrium.
