Light travels in straight lines
\n
When light hits an opaque object, it cannot pass through. This creates a shadow behind the object where no light reaches. The shadow forms because light travels in straight lines from the source.
\n
\n
Common Misconception
\n
Shadows are always black and the same size as the object. In reality, shadow size changes with distance and translucent objects create gray shadows.
